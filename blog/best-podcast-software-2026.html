Best Podcast Recording & Editing Software 2026

Published March 7, 2026 · By EditChoice Team · 12 min read

Podcasting continues to grow rapidly in 2026, with over 5 million active podcasts worldwide and advertising revenue surpassing $4 billion in the US alone. Whether you are launching your first show or upgrading your production workflow, choosing the right podcast recording and editing software is one of the most important decisions you will make. The wrong tool wastes hours every week; the right one makes production nearly effortless.

We spent three months testing every major podcast platform, recording dozens of episodes across each, and evaluating audio quality, editing speed, collaboration features, and overall value. This guide covers everything from all-in-one cloud platforms like Riverside and Descript to traditional desktop DAWs like Audacity and Adobe Audition.

1. Riverside.fm — Best Overall for Remote Podcast Recording

Riverside has established itself as the gold standard for remote podcast recording in 2026. Unlike Zoom or Google Meet, Riverside records each participant's audio and video locally at full quality, then uploads the tracks separately. This means your recordings are not affected by internet quality during the session. You get up to 48kHz WAV audio and 4K video per guest.

Key Features

  • Local recording — each participant's audio is captured at full quality on their device, regardless of connection speed
  • Separate tracks — individual audio and video files for every guest, making post-production far easier
  • AI transcription — automatic transcripts with speaker labels, exportable as SRT, VTT, or TXT
  • Magic Clips — AI automatically identifies the best moments and creates short clips for social media
  • Live streaming — simultaneously stream your recording to YouTube, LinkedIn, Facebook, and Twitch
  • Browser-based — guests join via a link, no downloads required

Pricing

Riverside offers a free tier with 2 hours of recording per month and up to 3 participants. The Standard plan at $15/month gives you 5 hours and up to 8 participants. The Business plan at $24/month unlocks unlimited recording, live streaming, and API access.

Our take: If you record interviews or co-hosted shows remotely, Riverside is the clear winner. The local recording approach solves the biggest problem in remote podcasting: audio quality degradation over the internet. The AI clip generator alone saves hours of social media content creation per episode.

2. Descript — Best for Text-Based Podcast Editing

Descript changed the game by letting you edit audio and video as easily as editing a text document. Record your podcast, and Descript transcribes it automatically. Delete a sentence from the transcript, and the corresponding audio is removed. Rearrange paragraphs, and the audio follows. For podcasters who dread waveform editing, this is transformative.

Key Features

  • Text-based editing — edit audio by editing the transcript; delete words, rearrange sections, fix mistakes
  • Studio Sound — AI-powered audio enhancement that removes background noise and improves vocal clarity in one click
  • Filler word removal — automatically detect and remove "um," "uh," "like," and other filler words
  • Overdub (AI voice cloning) — train a voice model on your recordings, then type corrections that sound like you
  • Multi-track editing — full support for separate guest tracks with individual processing
  • Publishing — publish directly to Apple Podcasts, Spotify, YouTube, and other platforms

Pricing

The free plan includes 1 hour of transcription per month. The Hobbyist plan at $24/month provides 10 hours of transcription and all core editing features. The Business plan at $33/month adds unlimited transcription, AI features, and team collaboration.

Our take: Descript is the fastest way to produce a polished podcast episode. The text-based editing paradigm eliminates the learning curve entirely. If you spend more time editing than recording, Descript will cut your production time in half. The AI voice cloning feature (Overdub) is particularly useful for fixing small mistakes without re-recording.

3. Audacity — Best Free Podcast Editor

Audacity has been the go-to free audio editor for over 20 years, and its 2026 release (version 3.7) brings significant improvements including a modernized interface, real-time effects preview, and non-destructive editing. It remains completely free and open-source, with no feature restrictions, no subscriptions, and no accounts required.

Key Features

  • Multi-track recording and editing — record from multiple inputs simultaneously
  • Extensive effects library — noise reduction, compression, equalization, normalization, and dozens more
  • Plugin support — VST, AU, LADSPA, and Nyquist plugins for unlimited expansion
  • Cross-platform — runs on Windows, macOS, and Linux
  • Non-destructive editing — new in 3.7, apply and remove effects without altering original audio
  • Macro automation — batch process multiple files with saved effect chains

Pricing

Completely free. No premium tiers, no feature gates. Audacity is funded by donations and maintained by a community of open-source contributors.

Our take: Audacity remains the best option for podcasters who want full control over their audio without spending a dime. The learning curve is steeper than Descript or GarageBand, but the depth of features rivals software costing hundreds of dollars. The noise reduction tool is particularly excellent for cleaning up recordings made in untreated rooms. 4. Adobe Audition — Best Professional Podcast Post-Production

Adobe Audition is the professional standard for audio post-production, used by major podcast networks including iHeartMedia, Wondery, and Gimlet. It offers a multitrack mixing environment, advanced spectral editing for surgical noise removal, and tight integration with Premiere Pro for video podcasts.

Key Features

  • Spectral frequency display — visually identify and remove specific noises (clicks, hums, room tone) with surgical precision
  • Multitrack mixing — layer voice, music, sound effects, and ads with professional routing
  • Match Loudness — batch normalize episodes to -16 LUFS (the podcast standard) or -14 LUFS for YouTube
  • Essential Sound panel — one-click presets optimized for dialogue, music, SFX, and ambience
  • Premiere Pro integration — round-trip editing between video and audio for video podcasts
  • Adaptive noise reduction — AI-powered noise floor removal that adapts to changing environments

Pricing

Adobe Audition is available as part of Adobe Creative Cloud at $22.99/month for the single app or $59.99/month for the full Creative Cloud suite. Annual plans offer a discount. If you are looking for free alternatives to the full Adobe suite, see our free Adobe alternatives roundup.

Our take: Audition is overkill for casual podcasters, but if you produce narrative podcasts with layered sound design, run a podcast network, or need broadcast-standard loudness compliance, nothing else comes close. The spectral editing alone justifies the subscription for anyone doing serious post-production work.

5. Zencastr — Best Simple Remote Recording

Zencastr focuses exclusively on making remote podcast recording dead simple. Guests click a link, join in their browser, and Zencastr records high-quality separate audio tracks (up to 48kHz WAV). The 2026 version adds automatic post-production that levels audio, removes background noise, and applies compression.

Key Features

  • Lossless separate tracks — WAV recordings for each guest at up to 48kHz
  • Automatic post-production — AI mixing, leveling, and noise removal with one toggle
  • Soundboard — trigger intro music, sound effects, and ad reads during recording
  • Live editing — trim and cut during or immediately after recording
  • Direct publishing — RSS hosting and distribution to Apple Podcasts and Spotify included

Pricing

The free Hobbyist plan allows 2 guests and 8 hours/month of recording. The Professional plan at $20/month unlocks unlimited guests, hours, and post-production features.

Our take: If you want the simplest possible remote recording experience without the complexity of Riverside's full production suite, Zencastr delivers. The automatic post-production feature is surprisingly good for a one-click solution, producing broadcast-ready audio from mediocre microphones.

6. Hindenburg Journalist — Best for Narrative Podcasts

Hindenburg Journalist was designed specifically for spoken-word audio production, and it shows. Every feature is optimized for voice: automatic loudness normalization, intelligent clip leveling, and a voice profiler that creates EQ presets from your recordings. It is the tool of choice for NPR, BBC, and documentary podcast producers.

Key Features

  • Voice Profiler — analyzes your voice and creates a custom EQ/compression preset
  • Automatic leveling — clips are automatically leveled as you add them to the timeline
  • Clipboard — visual panel for organizing audio clips before placing them on the timeline
  • Built-in publishing — export directly to podcast hosting platforms
  • Loudness metering — real-time LUFS metering with broadcast standard targets

Pricing

$95 one-time purchase for Hindenburg Journalist, or $12/month subscription. Hindenburg PRO for advanced users is $375 one-time or $25/month.

Our take: Hindenburg removes the complexity of a general-purpose DAW and gives you exactly what podcasters need. The automatic leveling alone saves significant time on every episode. If you produce narrative or interview shows and want opinionated software that makes good decisions for you, Hindenburg is excellent.

7. GarageBand — Best Free Option for Mac and iOS

GarageBand ships free with every Mac, iPad, and iPhone, making it the most accessible podcast editor for Apple users. While primarily marketed as a music production tool, GarageBand includes everything you need for basic podcast editing: multi-track recording, EQ, compression, noise gate, and a library of royalty-free jingles and sound effects.

Key Features

  • Podcast template — pre-configured tracks for voice, jingles, and sound effects
  • Built-in effects — compressor, EQ, noise gate, and vocal presets
  • Sound library — royalty-free music loops and jingles included
  • iPad/iPhone recording — record on mobile and continue editing on Mac via iCloud
  • Logic Pro upgrade path — GarageBand projects open seamlessly in Logic Pro when you need more power

Pricing

Free with any Apple device running macOS or iOS.

Our take: GarageBand is the perfect starting point for new podcasters on Apple hardware. It lacks advanced features like spectral editing or automatic transcription, but for recording, basic editing, and mixing, it handles the job well. 8. Podcastle — Best AI-Powered Podcast Platform

Podcastle merges recording, editing, and AI tools into a single browser-based platform. Its standout feature is Magic Dust, an AI audio enhancer that transforms recordings made on laptop microphones into professional-sounding audio. The platform also offers AI-generated voices for intro narration and text-to-podcast conversion.

Key Features

  • Magic Dust — AI audio enhancement that dramatically improves voice quality
  • AI voice skins — generate narration in realistic AI voices for intros or segments
  • Background noise removal — one-click removal with AI processing
  • Text-to-podcast — convert blog posts and articles into podcast episodes with AI voices
  • Remote recording — browser-based multi-guest recording with separate tracks

Pricing

Free tier with 1 hour of recording. Creator plan at $11.99/month. Business plan at $23.99/month with team features and API access.

Our take: Podcastle is the best option for solo podcasters who want AI to handle most of the production work. Magic Dust genuinely transforms poor audio quality into something listenable. The text-to-podcast feature is also useful for content repurposing.

9. Logic Pro — Best Advanced DAW for Mac Podcasters

Logic Pro is Apple's professional digital audio workstation, and while it is primarily a music production tool, its audio editing capabilities are exceptional for podcast production. Flex Time for timing corrections, advanced noise reduction, and a comprehensive mixer make it the DAW of choice for high-production podcasts on macOS.

Key Features

  • Advanced mixer — channel strips, sends, buses, and professional routing for complex shows
  • Flex Time and Flex Pitch — correct timing issues and pitch problems in voice recordings
  • Comprehensive plugin suite — Channel EQ, Compressor, Limiter, DeEsser, and more included
  • Spatial Audio — mix in Dolby Atmos for immersive podcast experiences
  • Low latency monitoring — record with effects in real-time without delay

Pricing

$199.99 one-time purchase from the Mac App Store. Free 90-day trial available. No subscriptions.

Our take: Logic Pro is the best investment for serious Mac podcasters who want professional-grade tools without a subscription. The one-time price is a bargain compared to monthly subscriptions that accumulate over years. If you already use GarageBand, the transition to Logic Pro is seamless.

10. Reaper — Best Budget DAW with Pro Features

Reaper is the hidden gem of digital audio workstations. At just $60 for a personal license, it offers capabilities that rival software costing ten times as much. Reaper is extremely customizable, lightweight, and runs on virtually any hardware. Its podcast community has created custom themes and action scripts specifically optimized for podcast workflows.

Key Features

  • Extremely lightweight — 15 MB installer, runs on old hardware, starts in seconds
  • Fully customizable — custom themes, toolbars, menus, and keyboard shortcuts
  • SWS extension — community extension adds loudness metering, auto-coloring, and batch processing
  • Unlimited tracks — no artificial limits on tracks, effects, or sends
  • Cross-platform — Windows, macOS, and Linux
  • Generous trial — full-featured 60-day trial with no restrictions

Pricing

$60 discounted personal license (for individuals or businesses earning under $20K/year from the software). $225 commercial license. Both include free updates through the next major version.

Our take: Reaper offers the best value in professional audio software. The $60 license gives you a DAW that can do everything Audition or Logic can do, and the customization options mean you can build a podcast editing workflow tailored exactly to your process.

How to Choose the Right Podcast Software

Selecting podcast software depends on your specific workflow, budget, and technical comfort level. Here is a decision framework:

  • Solo shows, minimal editing: GarageBand (Mac) or Audacity (any platform)
  • Interview shows recorded remotely: Riverside.fm or Zencastr
  • Maximum editing speed: Descript (text-based editing saves the most time)
  • Narrative podcasts with sound design: Adobe Audition or Hindenburg Journalist
  • AI-assisted production: Podcastle or Descript
  • Best value professional DAW: Reaper ($60) or Logic Pro ($199.99)

Recording Best Practices for 2026

Regardless of which software you choose, these recording practices will dramatically improve your podcast audio quality:

  1. Record in WAV or FLAC, not MP3 — always record in lossless format. Compress to MP3 only for final distribution.
  2. Use a sample rate of 44.1kHz or 48kHz at 24-bit depth — this captures far more dynamic range than 16-bit.
  3. Record separate tracks for each speaker — this gives you full control in post-production for leveling and noise removal.
  4. Set gain correctly — peaks should hit between -12dB and -6dB. Never let audio clip (hit 0dB).
  5. Normalize to -16 LUFS for podcast distribution — this is the standard loudness target for Apple Podcasts and Spotify.
  6. Record a 10-second room tone sample — silence at the start of each session helps noise reduction algorithms model your environment.

Final Verdict

Riverside.fm is our top overall pick for its unmatched remote recording quality and feature set. For editing speed, Descript is unbeatable with its text-based approach. If you need a free option, Audacity remains the most capable no-cost audio editor available. And for professional post-production, Adobe Audition is the industry standard.
